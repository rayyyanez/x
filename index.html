The First tweet Ever posted

In 2006, Jack Dorsey sent the first tweet: “just setting up my twttr,” marking the start of what would become Twitter, now rebranded as X. Originally a side project at the podcasting company Odeo, Twitter began as a simple SMS-based social network, limiting messages to 140 characters.

The platform quickly grew from a small experiment into a global phenomenon. By 2009, user numbers had skyrocketed, and in 2010, Twitter introduced Promoted Tweets as a revenue model. By 2013, it had over 200 million active users and a valuation exceeding $31 billion.

Today, X serves billions of users, providing real-time news, social interaction, and a platform for public discourse. Its role in politics, breaking news, and social movements highlighted during the 2016 U.S. election, the 2020 pandemic, and global protests has established it as a vital source of information worldwide.

Twitter's early simplicity laid the foundation for real-time global communication. Even in its first years, it showed how brief messages could connect people and share ideas instantly across the world.
